Top 20 Vietnamese Fonts for Web Design in 2026

2026-03-21
← Back to blog

Vietnamese typography is uniquely challenging. With 12 vowels, 5 tone marks, and diacritics that stack on top of each other, most fonts butcher the reading experience. Here are 20 fonts that actually get it right.

Why Vietnamese Fonts Are Hard

Vietnamese uses Latin script but adds a complex system of diacritical marks. A single character can carry TWO marks simultaneously — a vowel modifier AND a tone mark. If a font doesn't handle this well, characters collide, clip, or become unreadable.

DO NOT USE List

DO NOT USE for Vietnamese

These popular fonts have NO Vietnamese subset:

  • Poppins — No Vietnamese diacritics
  • DM Sans — Missing Vietnamese glyphs
  • Outfit — Latin only
  • Urbanist — No Vietnamese support
  • Sora — Missing tonal marks
  • Lato — Incomplete Vietnamese subset
  • Rubik — No Vietnamese diacritics

Always test with: Tôi đang học tiếng Việt, và tôi thấy rất thú vị!

Recommendation

For Vietnamese web projects in 2026: Be Vietnam Pro for body text, Montserrat or Comfortaa for headings. For ESL apps: Andika or Nunito for maximum diacritic clarity.
